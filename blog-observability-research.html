Monitoring & Alarm Strategy

Technical Design Review • By Nebat Taha

Download Full PDF

Overview: This document serves as a comprehensive evaluation of observability tools for EC2 and EKS environments. It analyzes the cost-efficiency, scalability, and operational overhead of native AWS services versus third-party and open-source ecosystems.

01. CloudWatch Alarms with SNS

Amazon CloudWatch Alarms are specifically designed to monitor AWS resources and trigger notifications when certain thresholds are reached. These alarms can be seamlessly integrated with Amazon Simple Notification Service (SNS) to automate alerts or take actions based on preset conditions.

Cost Analysis: CloudWatch costs are influenced by data volume ingested and the number of metrics. While basic monitoring is free, custom metrics and high-resolution alarms incur significant charges at scale. Optimization of ingestion filters is recommended.

02. Fluent Bit vs. CloudWatch Agent

Fluent Bit is a lightweight, open-source log processor popular in EKS. Its strength is filtering logs before forwarding, reducing "noise" and costs. While the CloudWatch Agent is optimized for native AWS integration, Fluent Bit excels in high-volume environments requiring advanced routing.

03. Prometheus, Grafana, & Alertmanager

Prometheus provides a powerful solution for time-series data. By leveraging PromQL, teams can identify trends in real time. Alertmanager handles the lifecycle, routing high-priority issues to PagerDuty and low-priority warnings to Slack to prevent alert fatigue.

04. Amazon Managed Prometheus (AMP)

AMP simplifies operation by handling scaling and security (IAM integration) without managing infrastructure. Note: AMP does not scrape metrics directly; it requires a collector like ADOT (AWS Distro for OpenTelemetry) to push metrics.

05. Elastic (ELK) Stack

The ELK stack (Elasticsearch, Logstash, Kibana) remains the robust choice for deep log analysis. While powerful for searching through terabytes of logs, it requires significant compute resources compared to the Prometheus/Loki ecosystem.

06. PagerDuty Integration

Integrating PagerDuty ensures real-time incident response. It centralizes monitoring from various sources, allowing for sophisticated on-call rotations and escalation policies that go beyond standard SNS emails.

07. Nagios

Nagios is a legacy powerhouse for infrastructure management. While highly extensible with plugins, its performance can be challenged as cloud environments scale dynamically, often making it better suited for static on-premise hardware monitoring.

08. Datadog

Datadog offers the "Gold Standard" of auto-discovery in Kubernetes. It monitors nodes, pods, and containers with zero-configuration in many cases. However, its pricing model is per-host/container, which can lead to "surprise" bills in highly elastic environments.
